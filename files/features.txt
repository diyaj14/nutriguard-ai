1. "Better Alternative" Suggestion Engine
When a product scores low, don't just say "bad for you" — show 2-3 better alternatives 
from your database ranked by the user's profile. This is the AHA moment judges love.
 Example: "This biscuit scores 3/10 for your diabetes profile. Here are 3 better snacks at
  this price range."

3. "Ingredient Deep Dive" with Red Flag Alerts
Beyond macros, flag specific harmful additives — things like high-fructose 
corn syrup, MSG, artificial sweeteners, trans fats, carrageenan. Show a simple 
traffic light per ingredient. This adds depth judges notice and users genuinely find useful.

4. Family Mode / Multi-Profile
Let one user set up profiles for their whole family — diabetic dad, 
peanut-allergic kid, fitness-focused mom. When scanning a product, show all family members'
scores at once with a "Safe for everyone?" summary.
 This is a strong social impact narrative and very demo-friendly.

 5. Scan History + Trend Dashboard
Show the user a weekly nutrition summary based on everything they've scanned — average 
sodium intake, sugar trend, protein hit rate vs. goal. This transforms a
 one-time tool into a habit-forming app, which judges love for feasibility/scalability scores.

 6. Regional Language Support + Indian Food Database
Most nutrition apps are built for Western foods. If this is targeting Indian 
markets (FSSAI is in your reference guide), adding support for regional 
language labels (Hindi, Tamil, etc.) and Indian packaged foods is a massive differentiator.
 Even showing the attempt scores huge on innovation and social impact.
